# Terms Of Service Draft

This is a product/legal draft for ChatMod Mobile planning. It is not final legal advice and should be reviewed before publishing, store submission, or public launch.

## 1. Service

ChatMod Mobile helps creators run a custom YouTube Live chat moderation bot from an Android phone. The app can connect to YouTube, read live chat messages for selected streams, send configured command or timer messages, and apply creator-configured moderation actions where YouTube permits them.

## 2. Account And Eligibility

- The creator is responsible for using a Google/YouTube account they are allowed to connect.
- The creator is responsible for making sure the bot channel has the correct YouTube moderator permissions before enabling moderation actions.
- ChatMod Mobile may refuse or limit access when a device session, entitlement, OAuth token, or backend configuration is invalid.

## 3. YouTube And Third-Party Platforms

ChatMod Mobile depends on YouTube APIs and Google OAuth. YouTube may change API behavior, quota, permissions, policies, or live chat availability. ChatMod Mobile is not responsible for outages, quota exhaustion, account restrictions, stream settings, or moderation results caused by YouTube or other third-party services.

Creators must comply with YouTube's terms, community guidelines, API policies, and any rules that apply to their channel or livestream.

## 4. Creator Content And Settings

The creator owns the commands, timers, rule presets, moderation notes, and settings they create. The creator is responsible for reviewing automated messages, blocked terms, rule thresholds, and moderation actions before and during a stream.

ChatMod Mobile should not be used to spam, harass, manipulate engagement, evade platform rules, or automate abusive behavior.

## 5. Acceptable Use

Creators may not use ChatMod Mobile to:

- Send spam, scams, phishing links, malware, or deceptive promotions.
- Harass, threaten, or discriminate against viewers.
- Evade YouTube enforcement, rate limits, or permissions.
- Collect chat data for unlawful purposes.
- Reverse engineer hosted services, attack infrastructure, or bypass entitlement checks.

## 6. Moderation Actions

Moderation automation can make mistakes. The creator is responsible for choosing rule settings, reviewing false positives, and confirming destructive actions where the app asks for confirmation. ChatMod Mobile should keep audit logs for moderation actions so creators can review what happened after a stream.

## 7. Data, Privacy, And Security

Data handling is described in `docs/PRIVACY_DRAFT.md`. ChatMod Mobile is designed to use minimum YouTube scopes, encrypted token storage on the backend, signed device sessions, account export/deletion controls, and local phone storage for runtime data.

Creators should not paste secrets, passwords, private keys, or sensitive personal information into commands, timers, feedback, support notes, or moderation notes.

## 8. Plans, Billing, And Free-Tier Beta

The current pricing decision is documented in `docs/PRICING.md`. A zero-cost beta may use local/starter entitlements and sideloaded builds. Paid features should not be promised until billing, restore purchase, cancellation, and entitlement verification are tested with real store credentials.

## 9. Availability And Changes

ChatMod Mobile may be unavailable because of phone battery settings, Android background restrictions, network loss, YouTube quota, backend hosting limits, or free-tier cold starts. The service may change as the product moves from prototype to beta to production.

## 10. Disclaimer

ChatMod Mobile is provided as-is during prototype and beta development. No guarantee is made that moderation will catch every harmful message, avoid every false positive, or remain available for every stream.
